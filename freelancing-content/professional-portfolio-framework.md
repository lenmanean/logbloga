# Professional Portfolio Framework

Guided exercises to build a professional portfolio that wins higher-ticket clients and supports $1,500–$4,000/month as a full-time freelancer.

## Purpose

A strong portfolio shows proof of results and positions you above commodity freelancers. For full-time work on Upwork and with direct clients, your portfolio needs to align with target clients, emphasize outcomes, and be easy to share in proposals and on your profile. This framework helps you structure positioning, case studies, presentation, and updates so you win more and higher-paying jobs.

## How to Use

- **Time:** 45–60 minutes  
- **When:** Complete early in Level 2 (Phase 1 of the [Implementation Plan](freelancing-level-2-plan.md)); align with your target clients and niche.  
- **AI tip:** Use ChatGPT or Claude to turn your bullet points into 2–3 sentence case studies; then edit for accuracy and tone.

Complete each part in order. Use the results in the [Portfolio Promotion Guide](freelancing-level-2-portfolio-promotion-guide.md) and in proposals (Proposal Templates ZIP in this level). Use the [Client Communication Templates](client-communication-templates.md) for consistent client-facing materials.

## Part 3: Presentation

### Professional layout and branding

- [ ] **Layout:** Clean, easy to scan; one project or case study per section or page  
- [ ] **Branding:** Consistent name, tagline, and (if used) logo or color  
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly:** Readable and navigable on phone (many clients view on mobile)  
- [ ] **Links:** Working links to live work, PDFs, or samples where relevant  
- [ ] **No typos:** Proofread titles and descriptions

## Part 4: Updates

### When to add and remove work

**Add new work:** After each strong project (with permission); at least every 2–3 months so portfolio stays current.

**Remove or refresh:** When a sample is outdated, no longer represents your best work, or is from a niche you're moving away from.
