# Proposal Outline

Use this structure for client proposals (Upwork, direct, or RFP). Customize with your service, niche, and rates. Use with [Contract Templates](../contract-templates/) for full engagement.

---

## 1. Intro (2–3 sentences)

- Who you are and what you do (service, niche, experience).
- Why you're a fit for this project or client (reference their goals or brief).
- One outcome you'll deliver (e.g. "I'll deliver a complete [deliverable] by [date] so you can [outcome].").

## 2. Scope

- **What's included:** List deliverables (e.g. 5 blog posts, 1 landing page, 2 rounds of revisions).
- **What's not included:** Anything out of scope (e.g. extra rounds, new scope without change order).
- **Assumptions:** What you need from the client (access, content, approvals by when).

## 3. Deliverables and timeline

- **Deliverable 1:** Description and due date.
- **Deliverable 2:** Description and due date.
- **Milestones:** e.g. Draft 1 by [date]; feedback by [date]; final by [date].

## 4. Investment

- **Option A (fixed):** Total project fee: $X. Payment: e.g. 50% upfront, 50% on delivery.
- **Option B (retainer):** $X/month for [scope]. Payment: monthly in advance.
- **What's included:** Revisions, meetings, or other terms.

## 5. Next steps

- **How to proceed:** e.g. "Reply to confirm scope and payment terms; I'll send the contract and invoice. We can start on [date] once the contract is signed and deposit received."
- **Contact:** Your name, email, calendar link.

---

**Tip:** Keep the proposal to 1–2 pages. Attach portfolio or samples if relevant. Use [Pricing Strategy Advanced](../pricing-strategy-advanced.md) to align rates with your level and niche.
