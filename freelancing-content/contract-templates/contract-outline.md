# Contract Outline (Key Clauses)

Use this as a structural outline for service agreements. Customize with your scope, rate, and terms. Use with Hello Bonsai or similar for e-signatures. **This is not legal advice; have a lawyer review your contract for your jurisdiction and situation.**

---

## 1. Parties and scope

- **Client:** Name, address (or company).
- **Freelancer:** Name, address (or business name).
- **Scope:** Brief description of services (or "as set out in Statement of Work / Proposal dated [date]").
- **Term:** Start date and end date (or "ongoing until terminated per Section X").

## 2. Payment

- **Rate or fee:** Fixed project fee, hourly rate, or monthly retainer.
- **Payment terms:** e.g. Net 15; due on receipt; 50% upfront and 50% on delivery.
- **Invoicing:** How often you invoice; what the client must do to pay (e.g. pay via link, check, ACH).
- **Late payment:** Interest or fee after X days; suspension of work after X days (if you choose to include).

## 3. Revisions and change orders

- **Revisions included:** e.g. 2 rounds of revisions per deliverable within scope.
- **Additional revisions or scope:** Extra fee; written change order or addendum.
- **Approval:** Client approves in writing (email or signed approval); timeline for feedback (e.g. 5 business days).

## 4. IP and work product

- **Deliverables:** Client owns final deliverables upon full payment (or per your policy).
- **Pre-existing IP:** You retain ownership of your tools, templates, and pre-existing work; you grant a license to use them in the deliverables.
- **Portfolio:** You may use the work in your portfolio unless the client requests confidentiality (and you agree).

## 5. Termination

- **For cause:** Either party may terminate for material breach with X days notice and opportunity to cure.
- **Convenience:** Either party may terminate with X days written notice (e.g. 14 or 30 days).
- **On termination:** Client pays for work completed up to the termination date; you deliver work product paid for; no refund of deposits (or prorate per your policy).

## 6. General (boilerplate)

- **Confidentiality:** Each party keeps the other's confidential information confidential (define what's confidential).
- **Independent contractor:** You are not an employee; no benefits; you're responsible for your own taxes and insurance.
- **Limitation of liability:** Cap on liability (e.g. total fees paid in the last 12 months) unless required by law.
- **Governing law and disputes:** State/country; how disputes are resolved (e.g. mediation, arbitration, small claims).

**Disclaimer:** This outline is for structure only. Laws vary by location and project type. Consult a qualified attorney for your contracts.
