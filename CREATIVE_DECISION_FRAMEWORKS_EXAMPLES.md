# Creative Decision Frameworks - Examples & Structure

## What Are Creative Decision Frameworks?

Creative decision frameworks are **guided exercises and prompts** that help users think through decisions we cannot make for them:
- Choosing their niche
- Defining their brand identity
- Generating business ideas
- Selecting target audiences
- Creating value propositions

**Key Principle:** We don't tell them what to choose - we help them discover it through structured thinking.

## Example 1: Niche Selection Framework (Social Media Package)

### Structure: Multi-Step Decision Process

**Step 1: Brainstorming Phase**
```
List 10 niches you're interested in:
1. _________________________
2. _________________________
3. _________________________
... (continue to 10)

For each niche, rate on a scale of 1-10:
- Passion: How much do you enjoy this? (1-10)
- Expertise: How knowledgeable are you? (1-10)
- Market Demand: How many people need this? (1-10)
- Competition: How saturated is this? (1-10, lower = less competition)
- Monetization Potential: Can you make money? (1-10)

Calculate Total Score: Passion + Expertise + Market Demand + (10 - Competition) + Monetization
```

**Step 2: Validation Phase**
```
For your top 3 niches, answer these questions:

NICHE: _________________________

1. What specific problems do people in this niche face?
   - Problem 1: _________________________

   - Problem 3: _________________________

   - Content Type 2: _________________________
   - Content Type 3: _________________________

3. Who are 5 successful creators in this niche?
   - @_________________________ (What do they do well?)
   - @_________________________ (What gaps can you fill?)
   - @_________________________ (What's your unique angle?)
   - @_________________________
   - @_________________________

4. What's your unique perspective?
   - What makes you different? _________________________
   - What's your "secret sauce"? _________________________
```

**Step 3: Final Decision**
```
Based on your scores and validation:

My chosen niche: _________________________

Why this niche? (Write 2-3 sentences)
_________________________________________
_________________________________________

_________________________________________
```

## Framework Design Principles

### 1. Progressive Disclosure
- Start broad (brainstorming)
- Narrow down (validation)
- Finalize (decision)

### 2. Multiple Options
- Never force one answer
- Provide 3-5 options to consider
- Let users choose their path

### 3. Scoring/Rating Systems
- Quantify decisions when possible
- Make comparisons easier
- Remove emotional bias

### 4. Real-World Validation
- Research competitors
- Check market demand
- Validate assumptions

### 5. Examples & Tips
- Include examples in sidebars
- Provide tips for each section
- Show "good" vs "bad" examples

---

## Delivery Formats

### Option 1: Fillable PDF
**Pros:**
- Users can print and fill out
- Works offline
- Familiar format

**Cons:**
- Not interactive
- Can't save progress easily
- Static content

### Option 2: Interactive Web Page
**Pros:**
- Can save progress
- Dynamic validation
- Progress tracking
- Can link to resources

**Cons:**
- Requires development
- Needs internet connection
- More complex to build

### Option 3: Hybrid Approach (Recommended)
- **PDF worksheets** for offline work
- **Web form** for digital submission
- **Both formats** available

## Key Takeaways

1. **Frameworks guide, don't dictate** - Users make final decisions
2. **Multiple steps** - Break complex decisions into manageable parts
3. **Validation included** - Help users verify their choices
4. **Scoring systems** - Quantify qualitative decisions
5. **Real examples** - Show how others made similar decisions
6. **Progress tracking** - Users can see where they are in the process
7. **Flexible formats** - PDF for offline, web for interactive

These frameworks help users think through creative decisions systematically, leading to better, more confident choices.
