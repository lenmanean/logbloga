# Web Apps Level 2: Customer Acquisition Guide

## Overview

This guide covers strategies for acquiring customers for your SaaS MVP. Focus on cost-effective methods that work for bootstrapped startups.

## Acquisition Channels

### 1. Content Marketing

**Strategy**:
- Write blog posts solving customer problems
- Create how-to guides
- Share case studies
- Publish weekly newsletter

**Time Investment**: 5-10 hours/week
**Cost**: $0-50/month (tools)
**Expected Results**: 10-50 sign-ups/month

### 2. Social Media

**Platforms**:
- Twitter/X: Tech audience
- LinkedIn: B2B products
- Reddit: Niche communities
- Indie Hackers: Founder community

**Strategy**:
- Share updates and learnings
- Engage with your audience
- Provide value, don't just promote
- Join relevant communities

**Time Investment**: 1-2 hours/day
**Cost**: $0
**Expected Results**: 5-20 sign-ups/month

### 3. Product Hunt Launch

**Preparation** (2 weeks before):
- Create great product page
- Prepare screenshots/video
- Write compelling description
- Get friends ready to upvote

**Launch Day**:
- Submit at 12:01 AM PST
- Engage with comments
- Share on social media
- Thank supporters

**Expected Results**: 50-500 sign-ups (if successful)

### 4. Partnerships

**Types**:
- Complementary products
- Influencers in your niche
- Communities and forums
- Other solo founders

**Approach**:
- Offer mutual value
- Cross-promotion
- Affiliate programs
- Co-marketing

**Time Investment**: 2-5 hours/week
**Cost**: $0-200/month
**Expected Results**: 5-25 sign-ups/month

### 5. Paid Advertising (When Ready)

**Start Small**:
- Budget: $100-500/month
- Test Google Ads or social ads
- Focus on one channel
- Measure ROI carefully

**When to Scale**:
- Positive ROI
- Proven conversion
- Sufficient budget
- Optimized landing page

## Conversion Optimization

### Landing Page

**Elements**:
- Clear value proposition
- Social proof
- Feature highlights
- Strong CTA
- FAQ section

**A/B Testing**:
- Headlines
- CTAs
- Pricing display
- Testimonials

### Onboarding

**Goals**:
- Get to "aha!" moment quickly
- Reduce friction
- Provide guidance
- Show value immediately

**Steps**:
1. Welcome email
2. Quick tutorial
3. First success
4. Feature highlights

## Retention Strategies

### First 30 Days

**Week 1**:
- Welcome email series
- Onboarding support
- Feature tutorials
- Check-in call (optional)

**Week 2-4**:
- Usage tips
- Success stories
- Feature updates
- Community access

### Ongoing

- Regular check-ins
- Feature announcements
- Educational content
- User feedback requests

## Metrics to Track

### Acquisition Metrics

- Sign-ups per channel
- Cost per acquisition (CAC)
- Conversion rate
- Time to first value

### Engagement Metrics

- Daily active users
- Feature adoption
- Support tickets
- User feedback

### Revenue Metrics

- Trial-to-paid conversion
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Customer lifetime value (LTV)
- Churn rate

## Budget Allocation

### Free Channels (Start Here)

- Content marketing: $0
- Social media: $0
- Community engagement: $0
- Partnerships: $0

**Total**: $0/month

### Paid Channels (When Ready)

- Paid ads: $100-500/month
- Tools: $50-100/month
- Content creation: $0-200/month
- Events: $0-500/month

**Total**: $150-1,300/month

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Spreading Too Thin

**Problem**: Trying every channel
**Solution**: Focus on 1-2 channels, master them

### ❌ Not Tracking

**Problem**: Don't know what works
**Solution**: Set up analytics from day 1

### ❌ Ignoring Retention

**Problem**: Only focus on acquisition
**Solution**: Retention is cheaper than acquisition

## Success Criteria

You're succeeding when:

- [ ] Consistent sign-ups (10+/month)
- [ ] Positive unit economics
- [ ] Growing organically
- [ ] High trial-to-paid conversion
- [ ] Low churn rate

---

**Remember**: Customer acquisition is a marathon, not a sprint. Focus on sustainable growth!
